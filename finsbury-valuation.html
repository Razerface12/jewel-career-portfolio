The question

Why this company, and what's being tested

In September 2023, DBAY Advisors agreed to take Finsbury Food Group private at 110p per share (£143.4m) — a 24% premium to the undisturbed price, yet two institutional shareholders publicly argued the company was undervalued. This model rebuilds Finsbury's economics from its filings to answer one question: what was a share worth on a fundamental, discounted-cash-flow basis?

Headline finding
162p / share

Discounting unlevered free cash flow at 8.5% WACC and 2% terminal growth implies an equity value of ~£212m — roughly 48% above the 110p deal. Even at a punitive 10% WACC and 1.5% growth, the model returns ~121p — still above the offer. Consistent with the shareholders who opposed the take-private as too cheap.

How it's built

Methodology

  • Linked statements: Assumptions → Income Statement → Cash Flow → Balance Sheet → DCF.
  • Every output is a live Excel formula — change an input, the valuation re-flows.
  • Unlevered FCF = EBIT×(1−tax) + D&A − capex − ΔNWC.
  • WACC built bottom-up via CAPM and after-tax cost of debt.
  • Condensed balance sheet balances every year by construction.

Data integrity

What's real vs estimated

Reported figures (revenue, EBITDA, PBT, net debt, margins, DPS) come from Finsbury's published reports and are flagged blue with source comments in the workbook. All other line items and forecasts are clearly-labelled analyst estimates.
