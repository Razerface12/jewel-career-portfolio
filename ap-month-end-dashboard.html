About This Project
What this demonstrates and how it was built

This model was built to demonstrate competency across the core transactional finance functions central to an operational finance analyst role. All data is illustrative and constructed from first principles — no proprietary data is used.

📄

AP Invoice Management

Demonstrates understanding of the full AP cycle: invoice receipt, PO matching, approval workflow, aging analysis, and escalation logic for overdue items. Mirrors the purchase-order limit monitoring and supplier liaison required in a finance analyst role.

📅

Month-End Close Process

Task tracker built to replicate the actual sequence of a period-end close: journals first (payroll, depreciation, prepayments), reconciliations second, management reporting third. Priority-ordered to reflect real close deadlines.

🏦

Bank Reconciliation

Shows the two-sided reconciliation approach — adjusting the bank statement balance for timing differences (uncleared BACS/CHAPS) and confirming the ledger balance matches. Demonstrates awareness of BACS vs CHAPS clearing timelines.

📊

Prepayment Schedule

Balance sheet reconciliation of prepaid assets: correctly amortising each contract over the service period with monthly journal entries. The debit/credit table shows the double-entry correctly with charges to the relevant P&L expense lines.

⚠

Escalation Logic

Two invoices are intentionally flagged at 90+ days — one with a disputed coverage period, one with a PO limit breach. Realistic close-cycle problems that require Finance Manager involvement, showing awareness of when to escalate vs resolve independently.

🔗

Multi-function Linkage

The model is internally consistent: the Orbit Insurance prepayment (insurance expense) links to the overdue Orbit AP invoice (£4,200) — the same supplier appears in both the aging analysis and the prepayment schedule, as they would in a real system.
