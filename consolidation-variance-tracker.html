Multi-Entity Consolidation & Variance Tracker

When two finance functions merge, the first casualty is a single, trustworthy view of performance. This tool consolidates site-level P&L across two legacy businesses into one group report, flags every material variance against budget, writes the commentary automatically, tracks delivery of cost-saving initiatives, and catches data-quality errors before they reach the board pack — the day-one work of post-merger reporting.

● Synthetic data — built to demonstrate method, not to report real figures

Consolidated P&L

Rolled up from site level · Actual vs Budget · variances flagged at the materiality threshold above.

Site performance

Operating profit by site — Actual vs Budget. Hover a bar for detail.

Auto-generated commentary

Narrative written from the variance data — reranks as you change scope or threshold.

Site-level detail

Every operating site in scope, ranked by adverse operating-profit variance.

Cost-synergy & initiative tracker

Post-merger, savings are promised to the market. This tracks each initiative's realised value against its run-rate target — the evidence a board needs that the synergy case is landing.

Data-quality checks

Automated validation run before consolidation — the same discipline that stops a mis-keyed figure or a mis-classified cost reaching leadership.

    Method. All figures are synthetic. The tool performs a real bottom-up consolidation (site → legacy entity → group), computes variance to budget in £ and %, applies a dual materiality gate (absolute £ and % of line), ranks drivers by £ impact to write the commentary, tracks initiative realisation against target, and runs pre-consolidation validation rules (missing baseline, sign/logic errors, out-of-range cost ratios). Nothing is hard-coded — change the controls and every number, flag and sentence recomputes.
    Built by Jewel Thomas — Financial Analyst · ACCA Part-Qualified · portfolio. Portable to any multi-site or multi-entity finance function (post-merger integration, multi-depot FMCG, franchise estates, shared-service consolidation).
